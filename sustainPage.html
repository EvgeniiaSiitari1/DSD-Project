    About

    In looking at what is sustainable fashion, we have recognised three important, large dimensions relating to the impact that fashion has: Economic Impact, Environmental Impact, and Social Impact. We have broken these large dimensions to six smaller themes to be able to analyse each brand and whether they fill our minimum standards. The selected themes include:.

    • Transparent Value Chain

      Must have: Design country, material and its origin as well as production country are known Supply chain known and supplier data gathered Auditing of suppliers

    • Sustainable Materials

      Must-have: No fur used, and no hazardous chemicals used in production of materials / Use of hazardous chemicals in production chain minimized* Long-lasting / reparable / recycled / natural / organic / easily recyclable materials Certificated materials

    • Lower Carbon FootPrint

      Must-have: Energy-efficient production & efficient logistics Renewable energy used / flights avoided Carbon footprint defined and plan for emission reduction & compensation implemented

    • Design in Circular Economy

      Must-have: Design for quality and long-term use Zero waste / wastewater or waste of production used by another company Offering repair / 2nd hand / renting / recycling services

    • Good Working Conditions

      Must-have: No child or forced labor, no unsafe or unfair working conditions Living wage commitments and decent working hours Code of conduct / offers education

    • Responsible Online Shopping

      Must-have: No incineration or landfilling of returned items Products are packed in natural or recycled materials Environmentally-friendly delivery options

    Aatise

    Two Eco Fashion Activists who have challenged themselves to offer ethical, sustainable and beautiful clothing. It all starts with a shared passion for fashion. Since childhood, this universe has inspired us and fulled our imagination. Surrounded by our mothers and grandmothers who sewed their own clothes, we sailed between the scraps of cloth, the threads and the needles and imagined the clothes we would wear, once grown up. Creations that would say something about us and our aspirations.

    ARYS

    ARYS designs progressive pieces for all gender, combining the practical fabrics you need for an active lifestyle with futuristic and contemporary forms. Founded in 2014, ARYS has since won multiple awards and gained success in Japan. Their fusion of fashion and function has long been a big trend in Asia and now ARYS is bringing this trend to Europe.
